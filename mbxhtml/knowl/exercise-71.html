EXTRA 6 \thomasee{See 11.6: 27-34}Consider the hyperbola \(\frac{x^2}{a^2}-\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1.\) Construct a box centered at the origin with corners at \((a, \pm b)\) and \((-a,\pm b)\text{.}\) Draw lines through the diagonals of this box. Rewrite the equation of the hyperbola by solving for \(y\) and then factoring to show that as \(x\) gets large, the hyperbola gets really close to the lines \(y=\pm \frac{b}{a}x\text{.}\)

Hint

rewrite so that you obtain \(y=\pm\frac{b}{a}x\sqrt{\text{ something } }\)

. These two lines are often called oblique asymptotes.

Now apply what you have just done to sketch the hyperbola \(\frac{x^2}{25}-\frac{y^2}{9}=1\) and give the location of the foci.
