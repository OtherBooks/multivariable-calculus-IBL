The volume of a box is \(V(x,y,z)=xyz\text{.}\) Imagine that each variable depends on \(t\text{.}\)

  1. If both \(y\) and \(z\) remain constant we only need to replace \(x\) with \(x(t)\)

    1. State the new equation for \(V\)

    2. What is \(dV/dt\text{?}\)

    3. Times both sides by \(dt\) to obtain a formula for \(dV\) when all bur \(x\) is constant.

  2. Repeat Part 1 for when \(y\) is the only non constant variable, and then for when \(z\) is the only non constant variable.

  3. What is \(dV/dt\) in terms of \(dx/dt\text{,}\) \(dy/dt\text{,}\) and \(dz/dt\) when all three variables are not constant.

  4. EXTRA 3 The matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}yz\amp ?\amp ? \end{bmatrix}\) is the derivative. The columns we'll call the partial derivatives. The partial derivatives make up the whole. Show that we can write \(dV\) as the matrix product (fill in the blanks) \begin{equation*} dV = \begin{bmatrix}yz\amp ?\amp ? \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}dx\\dy\\dz \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*} How do the columns of this matrix relate to the previous portions of the exercise.
