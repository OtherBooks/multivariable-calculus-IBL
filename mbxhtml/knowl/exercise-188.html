Exercise10.2.6

EXTRA 18 See Sage. Think of an airplane wing as you solve this exercise. Consider the vector field \(\vec F=(2x-y,x)\text{.}\) Let \(C\) be the curve that starts at \((-2,0)\text{,}\) follows a straight line to \((1,3)\text{,}\) and then back to \((-2,0)\) along the parabola \(y=4-x^2\text{.}\)

  1. Look at a drawing of \(C\) and the vector field (see margin for the Sage link). If we go counterclockwise around \(C\text{,}\) for each part of \(C\text{,}\) guess the signs of the counterclockwise circulation and the flux (positive, negative, zero).

  2. Find the flux of \(\vec F\) across \(C\text{.}\) There are two curves to parametrize. Make sure you traverse along the curves in the correct direction. Hint

    You should get integer values along both parts. Check your work with Sage, but make sure you show us how to do the integrals by hand.
