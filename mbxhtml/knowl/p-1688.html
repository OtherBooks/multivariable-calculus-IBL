EXTRA 11 Watch a YouTube video.Suppose a planar region \(R\) has density \(\delta(x,y)\text{.}\). The inertia about a line \(L\) we know is \(I_x=\iint_R d^2 \delta(x,y)dA\text{,}\), where \(d\) is the radius of rotation (distance to the line \(L\)). What constant radius \(R\) should we replace the variable radius \(d\) with so that \(\int_C R^2 dm = \int_C d^2 dm\text{.}\). Explain why the radius of rotation about the \(z\)-axis is \begin{equation*} R_z = \sqrt{\frac{\iint_R (x^2+y^2)\delta(x,y)dA}{\iint_R \delta(x,y)dA}}. \end{equation*}
