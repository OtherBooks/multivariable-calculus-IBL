Challenge10.5.1

Suppose a wire with density \(\delta(x,y)=x^2+y\) lies along the curve \(C\) which is the upper half of a circle around the origin with radius \(7\text{.}\)

  1. Parametrize \(C\) (find \(\vec r(t)\) and the domain for \(t\)).

  2. Where is the wire heavier, at \((7,0)\) or \((0,7)\text{?}\) [Compute \(\delta\) at both.]

In <<Unresolved xref, reference "semicircle_centroid"; check spelling or use "provisional" attribute>>, we showed that the centroid of the wire is \((\bar x, \bar y) = \left(0,\frac{2(7)}{\pi})\right)\text{.}\) We now seek the center of mass.

  1. Before computing:

    1. Will \(\bar x\) change? Will \(\bar y\) change?

    2. How will each change?

    3. Explain. Hint

      Read the three paragraphs before this exercise!

  2. Set up the integrals needed to find the center of mass. Then use technology to compute the integrals. Give an exact answer (involving fractions), rather than a numerical approximation.

  3. Change the radius from 7 to \(a\text{,}\) and guess what the center of mass will be. (This is why you need the exact answer above, not a numerical answer).
