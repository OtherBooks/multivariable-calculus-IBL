Consider the function \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\text{.}\) Consider the level curve \(C\) given by \(f(x,y)=25\text{.}\) Our goal is to find an equation of the tangent line to \(C\) at \(P=(3,-4)\text{.}\)

  1. Draw \(C\text{.}\)

  2. Compute \(\vec \nabla f\) and add to your graph the vector \(\vec \nabla f(P)\text{.}\)

We know the point \(P=(3,-4)\) is on the tangent line. Let \(Q=(x,y)\) represent another point on the tangent line.

  1. Add to your graph the point \(Q\) and the vector \(\vec {PQ} = (x-3,y+4)\text{.}\)

  2. Why does the dot product \(\vec \nabla f(P)\cdot\vec{PQ}\) equal zero?

  3. Compute this dot product and explain why this an equation of the tangent line?
