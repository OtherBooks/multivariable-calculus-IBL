Item10.5.6.3

  • Now suppose the mass of the object at point \(P_1\) is 2 kg, and the mass of the object at point \(P_2\) is 5 kg.

    1. Make a hypothesis (no calculations) about if the center of mass will be closer to \(P_1\) or \(P_2\text{?}\)

    2. Give a physical reason for your answer before doing any computations.

    3. Now find the center of mass \((\bar x, \bar y, \bar z)\) of the two points. Hint

      Think of it as a weighted average. If you are still stuck, see the margin

      EXTRA 6 Hint

      You should get \(\bar x= \frac{2x_1+5x_2}{2+5}\text{.}\)
