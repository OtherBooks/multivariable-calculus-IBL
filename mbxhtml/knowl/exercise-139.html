Exercise8.3.4Tangent Plane General Formula

Consider the function \(z=f(x,y)\text{.}\) Explain why an equation of the tangent plane to \(f\) at \((x,y)=(a,b)\) is given by \begin{equation*} z-f(a,b) = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(a,b) (x-a) + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(a,b) (y-a). \end{equation*}

Then give an equation of the tangent plane to \(f(x,y) = x^2+3xy\) at \((3,-1)\text{.}\)

Hint

Use either differential notation or a parametrization. Try both ways.
