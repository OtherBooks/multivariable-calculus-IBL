You only needed to show how to obtain the radius of gyration about the \(z\) axis. All three radii of gyration are found using the formulas \begin{equation*} R_x = \sqrt{\frac{\iint_R (y^2+z^2)dm}{\iint_R dm}}, R_y = \sqrt{\frac{\iint_R (x^2+z^2)dm}{\iint_R dm}}, \text{ and } R_z = \sqrt{\frac{\iint_R (x^2+y^2)dm}{\iint_R dm}}. \end{equation*} where \(dm=\delta(x,y) dA\text{.}\). Note that in 2 dimensions, we have \(z=0\text{,}\), so the formulas for \(R_x\) and \(R_y\) are simpler.
