In the definitions above, we could have replaced the circle \(C_a\) with a square of side lengths \(a\) centered at \((x,y)\) with interior area \(A_a\text{.}\). Alternately, we could have chosen any collection of curves \(C_a\) which “shrink nicely” to \((x,y)\) and have area \(A_a\) inside. Regardless of which curves you chose, it can be shown that \begin{equation*} N_x-M_y=\lim_{a\to 0} \frac{1}{A_a}\oint_{C_a} \vec F \cdot \vec T ds \text{ and } M_x+N_y=\lim_{a\to 0} \frac{1}{A_a}\oint_{C_a} \vec F \cdot \vec n ds. \end{equation*}
