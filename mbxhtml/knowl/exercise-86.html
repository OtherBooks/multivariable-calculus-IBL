Exercise5.1.14

For each curve below, set up an integral formula which would give the length, and sketch the curve. Do not worry about integrating them. EXTRA 7 The reason I don't want you to actually compute the integrals is that they will get ugly really fast. Try doing one in Wolfram Alpha and see what the computer gives.

  1. The parabola \(\vec p(t) = (t,t^2)\) for \(t\in[0,3]\text{.}\)

  2. The ellipse \(\vec e(t) = (4\cos t,5\sin t)\) for \(t\in[0,2\pi]\text{.}\)

  3. The hyperbola \(\vec h(t) = (\tan t,\sec t)\) for \(t\in[-\pi/ 4,\pi/4]\text{.}\)
