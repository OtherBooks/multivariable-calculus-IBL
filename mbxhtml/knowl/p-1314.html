EXTRA 6 Watch a YouTube video. Suppose \(f\) is a continuously differentiable function, defined along some open region containing the smooth curve \(C\text{.}\). Let \(\vec r(t)\) be a parametrization of the curve \(C\) for \(t\in[a,b]\text{.}\). Then we have \begin{equation*} f(\vec r(b))-f(\vec r(a))=\int_a^b Df(\vec r(t))D\vec r(t)\ dt. \end{equation*}
