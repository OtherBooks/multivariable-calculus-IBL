Exercise8.3.1Tangent Lines

Consider the function \(y=f(x)=x^2\text{.}\)

  1. The derivative is \(f'(x) = ?\text{.}\)

    1. At the point \(x=3\) the derivative is \(f'(3)=?\)

    2. and the output \(y\) is \(y=f(3)=?\text{.}\)

If we move from the point \((3,f(3))\) to the point \((x,y)\) along the tangent line, then a small change in \(x\) is \(dx=x-3\text{.}\)

  1. What is \(dy\) in terms of \(y\text{?}\)

Differential notation states that a change in the output \(dy\) equals the derivative times a change in the input \(dx\text{,}\) which gives us the equation \(dy=f'(3)dx\text{.}\)

  1. Replace \(dx\text{,}\) \(dy\text{,}\) and \(f'(3)\) with what we know they equal, to obtain an equation \(y-?=?(x-?)\text{.}\)

  2. What does this equation represent?

  3. Draw both \(f\) and the equation from the previous part on the same axes.
