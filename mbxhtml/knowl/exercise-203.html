Exercise10.5.5

\larsonfive{ EXTRA 4 See Larson 15.2:23–26 for more practice.} A wire lies along the straight segment from \((0,2,0)\) to \((1,1,3)\text{.}\) The wire's density (mass per unit length) at a point \((x,y,z)\) is \(\delta(x,y,z)=x+y+z\text{.}\)

  1. Find the wire's density at \((0,2,0)\) and \((1,1,3)\text{.}\)

  2. Is the wire heavier at \((0,2,0)\) or at \((1,1,3)\text{?}\)

  3. What is the total mass of the wire? [You'll need to parametrize the line as your first step—see Exercise 3.1.15 if you need a refresher.]
