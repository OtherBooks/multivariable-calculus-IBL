Exercise9.2.5

EXTRA 7 You can remember \(\ds\frac{ds}{dt} = \left|\frac {d\vec r}{dt} \right|\) as follows. We use the differential \(ds\) to represents a change in distance, and \(dt\) represents a change in time. So the speed of an object is the change in distance \(ds\) over the change in time \(dt\text{.}\) Let \(\vec r(t)=(x,y,z)\) be a parametrization of a smooth space curve. Let \(\ds s(t)=\int_0^t \left|\frac {d\vec r}{d\tau} \right|\ d\tau\text{.}\)

  1. Explain why \(\ds\frac{ds}{dt} = \left|\frac {d\vec r}{dt} \right|\text{,}\) the speed.

  2. Now explain why \(s(t)\) is an increasing function.

Hint

Look up the fundamental theorem of calculus. To answer why is \(s\) increasing, what does “smooth” mean?
