Exercise12.3.1

Consider the polar change of coordinates \(x=r\cos\theta\) and \(y=r\sin\theta\text{,}\) which we could just write as \begin{equation*} \vec T(r,\theta)=(r\cos\theta,r\sin\theta). \end{equation*}

EXTRA 1 If you need a reminder of how to compute determinants, refer to Section 2.3.1

  1. Compute the derivative \(D\vec T(r,\theta)\text{.}\) You should have a 2 by 2 matrix.

We need a single number from this matrix that tells us something about area. Determinants are connected to area.

  1. Compute the determinant of \(D\vec T(r,\theta)\) and simplify.
