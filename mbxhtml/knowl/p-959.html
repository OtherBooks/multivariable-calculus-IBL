EXTRA 2 Watch a YouTube video. A space ship travels through the galaxy. Let \(\vec r(t) = (x,y,z)\) EXTRA 3 Technically, we should write \(\vec r(t) = (x(t),y(t),z(t))\text{.}\) However, we already know that \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) depend on \(t\text{,}\) hence we'll just leave the dependence on \(t\) off.be the position of the space ship at time \(t\text{,}\), with the earth at the origin \((0,0,0)\text{.}\).

  1. What are the velocity and speed of the space ship at time \(t\text{?}\) You answers should involve some derivatives (such as \(\frac{dx}{dt}\)).

  2. If the space ship travels for a really small time \(dt\text{,}\) then the speed is about constant. Since distance is speed times time, about how much distance (we'll call it \(ds\)) will the space ship travel in this short amount of time?

  3. As the ship travels from time \(t=a\) to time \(t=b\text{,}\) explain why the distance traveled (the arc length of the path followed) is \begin{equation*} s=\int_a^b |\vec r '(t)|\ dt = \int_a^b \sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{dt}\right)^2+\left(\frac{dy}{dt}\right)^2+\left(\frac{dz}{dt}\right)^2}\ dt . \end{equation*}
