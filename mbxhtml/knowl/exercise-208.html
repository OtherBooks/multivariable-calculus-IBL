Exercise11.1.2

Suppose \(\vec r(t)\) is a level curve of \(f(x,y)\text{.}\)

  1. Suppose you know that at \(t=0\text{,}\) the value of \(f\) at \(\vec r(0)\) is 7. What is the value of \(f\) at \(\vec r(1)\text{?}\) Hint

    What does it mean to be on a level curve?

  2. As you move along the level curve \(\vec r\text{,}\) how much does \(f\) change? What must \(\ds\frac{df}{dt}\) equal?

  3. At points along the level curve \(\vec r\text{,}\) we have the composite function \(f(\vec r(t))\text{.}\) Compute the derivative \(\ds\frac{df}{dt}\) using the chain rule.

  4. Use your work from the previous parts to explain why the gradient always meets the level curve at a 90\(^\circ\) angle.

We say that the gradient is normal to level curves (i.e., a gradient vector is orthogonal to the tangent vector of the curve).
