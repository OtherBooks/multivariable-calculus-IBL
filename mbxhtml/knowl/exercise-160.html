Exercise9.3.1

Sammy sits on a merry go round. He sits 3 feet from the center of the merry ground, and lets his big sister spin him around. We can parameterize Sammy's path with the vector equation \(\vec r(t) = (3\cos t, 3\sin t)\text{,}\) where \(t\) is in seconds. The point \((0,3)\) corresponds to the time \(t=\pi/2\text{.}\)

  1. Draw the curve for \(0\leq t\leq 2\pi\text{.}\)

  2. Compute \(\vec v\) and \(\vec a\text{.}\)

  3. At \((0,3)\) (so \(t=\pi/2\)), draw these two vectors. Are these two vectors orthogonal?

  4. Compute \(\vec T\) and \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt}\text{.}\)

  5. At \((0,3)\text{,}\) draw these two vectors. Are these two vectors orthogonal?

  6. Compute \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\) and \(\left|\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\right|\text{.}\)

  7. How is the length of \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\) related to the circle?
