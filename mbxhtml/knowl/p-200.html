EXTRA 4 \thomasee{See page 693.} \stewarts{See pages 801-802 if you are struggling}Let \(\vec u,\vec v\in\mathbb{R}^3\text{.}\). Let \(\theta\) be the angle between \(\vec u\) and \(\vec v\text{.}\).

  1. Use the law of cosines to explain why \(|\vec u-\vec v|^2=|\vec u|^2+|\vec v|^2-2|\vec u||\vec v|\cos\theta\text{.}\)

  2. Use the above together with problem 3.2.9 to derive \begin{equation*} \vec u\cdot \vec v=|\vec u||\vec v|\cos\theta. \end{equation*}
