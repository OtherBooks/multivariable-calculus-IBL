Suppose that \(\vec r(t)\) is a smooth parametrization of a moving object. Let \(\vec T\) be the unit tangent vector. The tangential component of acceleration and the normal component of acceleration are the scalars \(a_T\) and \(a_N\) that we obtain by writing the acceleration as the sum of a vector parallel to \(T\) and a vector orthogonal to \(\vec T\text{,}\), i.e. the scalars that satisfy \begin{equation*} \vec a = a_T\vec T+a_N\vec N. \end{equation*}
