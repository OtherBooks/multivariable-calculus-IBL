Definition9.4.5Binormal Vector

If \(\vec r\) is a parametrization of a smooth space curve with unit tangent vector \(\vec T\) and principle unit normal vector \(\vec N\text{,}\) then we define the binormal vector \(\vec B\) to be the cross product \begin{equation*} \vec B = \vec T\times \vec N. \end{equation*}

It's normal to both \(\vec T\) and \(\vec N\text{,}\) hence called the binormal vector.
