The symbols \(\{\) and \(\}\) are used to enclose sets, and the symbol \(|\) stands for “such that”. We read the above line as “\(R\) equals the set of \((x,y)\) such that zero is less than \(x\) which is less than 5, and 2 is less than \(y\) which is less than \(x+2\text{.}\)”
