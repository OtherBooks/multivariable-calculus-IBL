If the wire has density \(\delta\text{,}\), then the formulas for the center of mass are EXTRA 9 The quantity \(\int_C x dm\) is sometimes called the first moment of mass about the \(yz\)-plane (so \(x=0\)). Notationally, some people write \(M_{yz} =\int_C x ds\text{.}\) Similarly, we could write \(M_{xz}=\int_C y dm\) and \(M_{xy}=\int_C zdm\text{.}\) With this notation, we could write the center of mass formulas as \begin{equation*} (\bar x,\bar y,\bar z) = \left( \frac{M_{yz}}{m}, \frac{M_{xz}}{m}, \frac{M_{xy}}{m} \right) . \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} \bar x = \frac{\int_C x dm}{\int_C dm}, \bar y = \frac{\int_C y dm}{\int_C dm}, \text{ and } \bar z = \frac{\int_C z dm}{\int_C dm}, \text{ (Center of mass) } \end{equation*} where \(dm=\delta ds\text{.}\). Notice that the denominator in each case is just the mass \(m=\int_C dm\text{.}\).
