We are now prepared to approximate the volume using a first, second, and third order approximation.

  1. A first order approximation yields \(P=36\pi+ 36\pi\cdot 0.02 =36.72\pi.\) The volume increased by \(0.72\pi\) m\(^3\text{.}\)

  2. A second order approximation yields \begin{equation*} P=36\pi+ 36\pi\cdot 0.02 +12\pi (0.02)^2 =36.7248\pi. \end{equation*}

  3. A third order approximation yields \begin{equation*} P=36\pi+36\pi\cdot 0.02 +12\pi (0.02)^2+\frac{4}{3}\pi(0.02)^3 =36.724810\bar6\pi. \end{equation*}
