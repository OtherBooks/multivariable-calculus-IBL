Exercise10.1.1

An object moves from \(A=(6,0)\) to \(B=(0,3)\text{.}\) A parametrization of the objects path is \(\vec r(t) = (-6,3)t+(6,0)\) for \(0\leq t\leq 1\text{.}\) We are going to investigate what happens under a non-constant force.

  1. For \(0\leq t\leq .5\text{,}\) what is the displacement vector \(\vec{d}\) from \(\vec{r}(0) \rightarrow \vec{r}(0.5)\text{?}\) If the force encountered over this section is \(\vec F = (2,5)\) how much work is done?

  2. For \(.5\leq t\leq 1\text{,}\) what is the displacement vector \(\vec{d}\) from \(\vec{r}(0.5) \rightarrow \vec{r}(1)\text{?}\) If the force encountered over this section is \(\vec F = (2,6)\) how much work is done?

  3. How much total work was done?

  4. EXTRA 2 You can visualize what's happening in this exercise as follows. Attach a clothesline between the points (maybe representing two trees in your backyard). Put a cub scout space derby ship on the clothesline. Then the wind starts to blow. As the ship moves along the clothesline, the wind changes direction.Suppose that the force constantly changes as we move along the curve. At \(t\text{,}\) we'll assume we encountered the force \(F(t) = (2,5+2t)\text{.}\)

    1. Find \(\ds W=\int \vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_0^{0.5} (2,5+2t)\cdot (-6,3)dt.\) How does this compare to part (1)?

    2. Find \(\ds W=\int \vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_0^1 (2,5+2t)\cdot (-6,3)dt.\) How does this compare to part (3)?

  5. Explain why the total work done by this force along the path is \(\ds W=\int \vec F\cdot d\vec r\text{.}\) [What does \(\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\) represent?]
