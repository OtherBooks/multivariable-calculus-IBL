  • Explain why the work done by \(\vec F\) along the circle \(C\) EXTRA 4 We put the \(C\) under the integral \(\int_C\) to remind us that we are integrating along the curve \(C\text{.}\) This means we need to get a parametrization of the curve \(C\text{,}\) and give bounds before we can integrate with respect to \(t\text{.}\)is \begin{equation*} W = \int_C\left(-2y,2x\right)\cdot d\vec r = \int_0^{2\pi}\left(-2(3\sin t),2(3\cos t)\right)\cdot(-3\sin t, 3\cos t)dt. \end{equation*} Then integrate to show that the work done by \(\vec F\) along this circle is \(36\pi\text{.}\)
