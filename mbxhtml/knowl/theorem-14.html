Theorem13.3.1Stokes' Theorem

Suppose \(\vec F(x,y,z) = (M,N,P)\text{.}\) Suppose that \(S\) is a surface in the plane, whose boundary is the simple closed curve \(C\text{.}\) Suppose that \(\vec n\) is a continuous unit normal vector to \(S\) along the entire surface, where the orientation of \(\vec n\) matches the orientation of \(C\) according to the right hand rule (meaning that if you curl your right hand around \(\vec n\) with your thumb pointing along \(\vec n\text{,}\) then your fingers will follow the orientation of \(\vec C\)). Then Stokes' theorem states that \begin{equation*} \int_C\vec F\cdot d\vec r = \iint_S \text{ curl } \cdot \vec n d\sigma . \end{equation*}
