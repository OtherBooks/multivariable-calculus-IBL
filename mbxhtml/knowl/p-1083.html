Find the area of a parallelogram with corners \((0,0,0)\text{,}\), \((1,0,2)\text{,}\), and \((1,1,3)\text{,}\), and \((2,1,5)\text{.}\). See  3 We find area of a parallelogram by the vectors that form the edges. Since one point is the origin (subtracting it from the 2nd and 3rd points won't change anything), we compute the cross product \((1,0,2)\times (1,1,3) = (-2,-1,1)\text{.}\) The magnitude of the cross product is the area \(A = \sqrt{4+1+1}=\sqrt{6}\text{.}\).
