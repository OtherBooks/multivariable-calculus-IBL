EXTRA 2 Ask me in class to give you an informal picture approach that explains why \(dxdy=rdrd\theta\text{.}\) If we use the polar coordinate transformation \(x=r\cos\theta, y=r\sin\theta\text{,}\), then we can switch from \((x,y)\) coordinates to \((r,\theta)\) coordinates if we use \begin{equation*} dxdy=|r|drd\theta. \end{equation*}
