What is the cross product of \((1,2,3)\) and \((2,4,6)\text{?}\)? If two vectors are parallel, then what is their cross product? In particular, what is the cross product of \(\vec N\) and \(\vec \kappa\text{?}\)? See  8 The cross product of parallel vectors is always the zero vector \((0,0,0)\text{.}\) This is because the area of the parallelogram formed using the parallel vectors is always zero. So all three answers are \((0,0,0)\text{.}\) for an answer.
