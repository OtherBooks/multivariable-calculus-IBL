Exercise11.2.8

Suppose that \(f(x,y)\) has a critical point at \((a,b)\text{.}\)

  1. Find a general formula for the eigenvalues of \(D^2f(a,b)\text{.}\) Your answer will be in terms of the second partials of \(f\text{.}\)

  2. Let \(D=f_{xx}f_{yy}-f_{xy}^2\text{.}\)

    • If \(D\lt 0\text{,}\) explain why \(f\) has a saddle point at \((a,b)\text{.}\)

    • If \(D=0\text{,}\) explain why the second derivative test fails.

    • If \(D>0\text{,}\) explain why \(f\) has either a maximum or minimum at \((a,b)\text{.}\)

    • If \(D>0\text{,}\) and \(f_x(a,b)>0\text{,}\) does \(f\) have a local max or local min at \((a,b)\text{.}\) Explain.

  3. The only critical point of \(f(x,y) = x^2+3xy+2y^2\) is at \((0,0)\text{.}\) Does this point correspond to a local maximum, local minimum, or saddle point? Give the eigenvalues (which should come instantly out of part 1). Find \(D\text{,}\) from part 2, to answer the question.
