EXTRA 7 \thomasee{See 12.5: 13-20.} \stewarts{12.5: 6-15} Let \(P=(3,1)\) and \(Q=(-1,4)\text{.}\).

  1. Write a vector equation \(\vec r(t)=(x,y)\) for (i.e, give a parametrization of) the line that passes through \(P\) and \(Q\text{,}\) with \(\vec r(0)=P\) and \(\vec r(1)=Q\text{.}\)

  2. Write a vector equation for the line that passes through \(P\) and \(Q\text{,}\) with \(\vec r(0)=P\) but whose speed is twice the speed of the first line.

  3. Write a vector equation for the line that passes through \(P\) and \(Q\text{,}\) with \(\vec r(0)=P\) but whose speed is one unit per second.
