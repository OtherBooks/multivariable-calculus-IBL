Footnote12.2.4
Compare the two formulas \(KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2\) and \(KE = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2\text{.}\). If we replace speed with angular speed, then we replace mass with inertia. Heavy objects are hard to push. Objects with large inertia are hard to rotate. Just as mass is an objects resistance to being moved, inertia is an objects resistance to being rotated. Engineers build I-beams so that a large portion of the mass is far from the axis of rotation. This causes a large inertia, which prevents I-beams from rotating. in-context
