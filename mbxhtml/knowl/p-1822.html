Paragraph

You should have an upside down paraboloid, with at least 6 different parabolas drawn on the surface. These parabolas should divide the surface up into a bunch of different patches. Our goal is to find the area of each patch, where each patch is almost like a parallelogram.

  1. Find \(\ds \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial x}\) and \(\ds\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial y}\text{.}\)

  2. At the point \((2,1)\text{,}\) draw both vectors.

  3. These vectors form the edges of a parallelogram. Add that parallelogram to your picture.

  4. Show that the area of a parallelogram whose edges are the vectors \(\ds\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial x}(x,y)\) and \(\ds\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial y}\) is \(\sqrt{1+4x^2+4y^2}\text{.}\) Hint

    What was one part of the definition/meanings for the cross product.

  5. Find the area of the parallelogram whose edges are the vectors \(\ds\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial x}dx\) and \(\ds\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial y}dy\text{,}\) where \(dx\) and \(dy\) are not yet determined.
