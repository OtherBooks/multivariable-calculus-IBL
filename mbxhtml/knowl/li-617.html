Item10.1.7.5

  • (Challenge, we'll discuss in class.) Suppose \(\vec r\) is a counterclockwise parametrization of a closed curve. The outward normal vector would always point to the right as you move along the curve. Prove that \((dy,-dx)\) always points to the right of the curve. Hint

    If you want a right pointing vector, what should \(\vec B=\vec T\times \vec n\) always equal (either \((0,0,1)\) or \((0,0,-1)\)). Use the fact that \(\vec B\times \vec T = \vec n\) to get \(\vec n\text{.}\)
