Exercise8.3.3

Let \(z=f(x,y)=9-x^2-y^2\text{.}\) We'll parameterize this function by writing \(x=x, y=y, z=9-x^2-y^2\text{,}\) or in vector notation we'd write \begin{equation*} \vec r(x,y) = (x,y,f(x,y)). \end{equation*}

  1. Compute \(\ds \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\) and \(\ds \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\text{.}\)

  2. Next evaluate these partials at \((x,y)=(2,1)\text{.}\) You should have \(f_x=-4\text{.}\)

  3. What does the number \(-4\) mean? What does the number \(f_y\) mean?

  4. Compute \(\ds \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial x}\) and \(\ds \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial y}\text{.}\)

  5. Now evaluate these partials at \((x,y)=(2,1)\text{.}\)

  6. What do these vectors mean? Hint

    Draw the surface, and at the point \((2,1,4)\text{,}\) draw these vectors. See the Sage plot.

  7. The vectors above are tangent to the surface. Use them to obtain a normal vector to the tangent plane, and then given an equation of the tangent plane. (You should compare it to your equation from exercise 8.3.2.)
