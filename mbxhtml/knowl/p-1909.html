The notation \(\vec \nabla \times \vec F\) gives a convenient way to remember the formula, as \begin{align*} \text{ curl } (\vec F)=\vec \nabla \times \vec F \amp =\det \begin{bmatrix} \vec i \amp \vec j \amp \vec k\\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\amp \frac{\partial}{\partial y}\amp \frac{\partial}{\partial z}\\ M\amp N\amp P \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial y}P-\frac{\partial}{\partial z}N,\frac{\partial}{\partial z}M-\frac{\partial}{\partial x}P,\frac{\partial}{\partial x}N-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}M\right) \end{align*}
