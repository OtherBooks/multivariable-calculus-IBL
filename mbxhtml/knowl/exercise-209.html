Exercise11.1.3

Suppose the temperature at a point in the plane is given by the function {\(T(x,y)=x^2-xy-y^2\)} degrees Fahrenheit. A particle is at \(P=(2,3)\text{.}\)

  1. Use differentials to estimate the change in temperature if the particle moves 1 unit in the direction of \(\vec u=\left(3,4\right)\text{.}\)

    1. Find the unit vector \(\hat{u}\text{.}\) If the particle moves 1 unit in the direction of \(\vec{u}\) what is \(dx\) and \(dy\text{?}\)

    2. Find \(D\vec{T}(x,y)\)

    3. Estimate the change using differential notation (set-up the exercise, then compute).

  2. What is the actual change in temperature if the particle moves 1 unit in the direction of \(\vec u=\left(3,4\right)\text{?}\)

    1. What is the particle's new position?

    2. What is the particle's new temperature?

    3. What is \(\Delta T\text{?}\)

  3. Use differentials to estimate the change in temperature if the particle moves about .2 units in the direction of \(\vec u=\left(3,4\right)\text{.}\) Hint

    Use the previous parts as guides
