Exercise11.1.4

Suppose that \(z=f(x,y)\) is a differentiable function (so the derivative is the matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}f_x\amp f_y \end{bmatrix}\)). Remember to use differential notation in this exercise.

  1. If \((dx,dy)=(1,0)\text{,}\) which means we've moved one unit in the \(x\) direction while holding \(y\) constant, what is \(dz\text{?}\)

  2. If \((dx,dy)=(0,1)\text{,}\) which means we've moved one unit in the \(y\) direction while holding \(x\) constant, what is \(dz\text{?}\)

  3. Consider the direction \(\vec u=(2,3)\text{.}\) Find a unit vector in the direction of \(\vec u\text{.}\) If we move one unit in the direction of \(\vec u\text{,}\) what is \(dz\text{?}\) [It's all right to leave you answer as a dot product.]
