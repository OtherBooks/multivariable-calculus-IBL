EXTRA 8 \thomasee{See 11.5: 29.}Recall that \(x=r\cos\theta\) and \(y=r\sin\theta\text{.}\). Suppose that \(r=f(\theta)\) for \(\theta\in[\alpha,\beta]\) is a continuous function, and that \(f'\) is continuous. Show that the arc length formula can be simplified to \begin{equation*} s=\int_{\alpha}^{\beta}\sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{d\theta}\right)^2+\left(\frac{dy}{d\theta}\right)^2} = \int_{\alpha}^{\beta}\sqrt{r^2+\left(\frac{dr}{d\theta}\right)^2} . \end{equation*}
