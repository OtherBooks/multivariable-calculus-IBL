Exercise9.3.9

Consider the helix \(\vec r(t)=(4\cos t,4\sin t, 3t)\text{.}\) EXTRA 6 Because the speed is constant, many of the computations are simplified. You can use the Sage link to check your work. Compute \(\vec v\text{,}\) \(\vec T\text{,}\) \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt}\text{,}\) and \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\text{.}\) Then do the following:

  1. Show that \(\vec T\) and \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt}\) are orthogonal. (Do this for any time \(t\text{.}\))

  2. At \(t=2\pi\text{,}\) compute \(ds/dt\text{,}\) \(\vec T\text{,}\) \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt}\text{,}\) and \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\text{.}\)

  3. Let's slow down how quickly we travel along the curve. Replace \(t\) with \(t/5\text{.}\) Then at \(t=10\pi\text{,}\) give \(ds/dt\text{,}\) \(\vec T\text{,}\) \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt}\text{,}\) and \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\) for the new curve \(\vec r_2(t) =(4\cos(\frac{t}{5}),4\sin(\frac{t}{5}), 3(\frac{t}{5}))\text{.}\)

  4. Give a unit vector that points in the same direction as \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt}\) or \(\dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}\text{.}\)
