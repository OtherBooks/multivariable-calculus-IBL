Let \(x\) be a real number and \(f\) and \(g\) be functions of a single real variable. Suppose \(f\) is differentiable at \(g(x)\) and \(g\) is differentiable at \(x\text{.}\). The derivative of \(f\circ g\) at \(x\) is \begin{equation*} (f\circ g)'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(f\circ g)(x) = f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x). \end{equation*}
