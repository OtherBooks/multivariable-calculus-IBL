Compute \(\ds \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2+4}}dx\text{.}\). See  13 Let \(u=x^2+4\text{,}\) which means \(du=2xdx\) or \(dx=\frac{du}{2x}\text{.}\) This means \begin{equation*} \ds \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2+4}}dx = \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{u}}\frac{du}{2x} = \frac{1}{2}\int u^{-1/2}du = \frac{1}{2}\frac{u^{1/2}}{1/2} = \sqrt{u} = \sqrt{x^2+4}. \end{equation*} .
