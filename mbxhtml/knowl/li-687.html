Item10.4.2.4

  • We found a constant \(\bar f\) so that the area under \(\bar f\) matched the area under \(f\text{.}\) In other words, we solved the equation below for \(\bar f\text{:}\) \begin{equation*} \int_a^b \bar f dx = \int_a^b f dx \end{equation*} Solve for \(\bar f\) symbolically (without doing any of the integrals). This quantity is called the average value of \(f\) over \([a,b]\text{.}\) Hint

    Remember \(\bar{f}\) is a constant
