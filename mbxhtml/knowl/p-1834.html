This definition tells us how to compute any surface integral. The steps are almost identical to the line integral steps.

  1. Start by getting a parametrization \(\vec r\) of the surface \(S\) where the bounds form a region \(R\text{.}\)

  2. Find a little bit of surface area by computing \(d\sigma =\left |\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial u} \times \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial v}\right| dudv.\)

  3. Multiply \(f\) by \(d\sigma\text{,}\) and replace each \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(z\) with what they equals from the parametrization.

  4. Integrate the previous function along \(R\text{,}\) your parameterization's bounds.
