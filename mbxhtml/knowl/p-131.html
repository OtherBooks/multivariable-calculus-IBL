EXTRA 2 \thomasee{See 12.2:23-24.} \stewarts{See 12.2:5-6} Consider the vectors \(\vec u=\langle 1,2 \rangle\) and \(\vec v=\left\lt 3,1\right>\text{.}\). Draw \(\vec u\text{,}\), \(\vec v\text{,}\), \(\vec u+\vec v\text{,}\), and \(\vec u-\vec v\) with their tail placed at the origin. Then draw \(\vec v\) with its tail at the head of \(\vec u\text{.}\).
