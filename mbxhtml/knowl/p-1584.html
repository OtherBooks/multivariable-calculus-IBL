EXTRA 2 The most common error on this exercise is to divide both sides of an equation by \(x\text{,}\) which could be zero. If you do this, you'll only get 2 ordered pairs.Find the gradient of \(f\) and the gradient of \(g\text{.}\). Then solve the system of equations that you get from the equations \begin{equation*} \vec \nabla f = \lambda \vec \nabla g, x^2+y^2=1. \end{equation*} You should obtain 4 ordered pairs \((x,y)\text{.}\).
