Consider the tetrahedron \(D\) in the first octant that is underneath the plane that intersects the coordinate axes in the three point \((a,0,0)\text{,}\), \((0,b,0)\) and \((0,0,c)\text{,}\), where you can assume that \(a,b,c>0\text{.}\).

  1. An equation of an ellipse that passes through \((a,0)\) and \((0,b)\) is \(\ds\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1\text{.}\)

  2. An equation of a line through these same two points is \(\ds\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{b}=1\text{.}\)

  3. An equation of an ellipsoid through the three points \((a,0,0)\text{,}\) \((0,b,0)\text{,}\) and \((0,0,c)\) is \(\ds\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}+\frac{z^2}{c^2}=1\text{.}\)

  4. Guess an equation of the plane through these same three points, and then verify that your guess is correct by plugging the 3 points into your equation. This will provide you with an extremely fast way to get an equation of a plane.

  5. Set up an iterated integral that would give the volume of \(D\text{.}\)

  6. If the density is \(\delta(x,y,z) = 3x+2yz\text{,}\) set up iterated integrals that would give the mass \(m\)\bmw{ and moment of inertia \(I_y\) about the \(y\)-axis}.
