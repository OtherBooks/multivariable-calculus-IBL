EXTRA 3 \thomasee{See 3.11:45-62.} \stewarts{See 3.10} The manufacturer of a spherical storage tank needs to create a tank with a radius of 3 m. Recall that the volume of a sphere is \(V(r) = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\text{.}\) No manufacturing process is perfect, so the resulting sphere will have a radius of 3 m, plus or minus some small amount \(dr\text{.}\) The actual radius will be \(3+dr\text{.}\)

  1. Find the differential \(dV\text{.}\) This should be a formula with \(dV\text{,}\) \(r\text{,}\) and \(dr\text{.}\)

  2. If the actual radius is 3.02

    1. What is \(r\text{?}\)

    2. What is \(dr\text{?}\)

  3. What is \(dV\) equal to? What does \(dV\) tell you about the volume of the manufactured sphere?
