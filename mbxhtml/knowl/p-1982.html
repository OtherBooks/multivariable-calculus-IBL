Note: You don't need to evaluate the integral, rather you just need to set them up.

  1. The region \(D\) under the surface \(z=y^2\text{,}\) above the \(xy\)-plane, and bounded by the planes \(y=-1\text{,}\) \(y=1\text{,}\) \(x=0\text{,}\) and \(x=4\text{.}\)

  2. The region \(D\) in the first octant that is bounded by the coordinate planes, the plane \(y+z=2\text{,}\) and the surface \(x=4-y^2\text{.}\)

  3. The pyramid \(D\) in the first octant that is below the planes \(\ds\frac{x}{3}+\frac{z}{2}=1\) and \(\ds\frac{y}{5}+\frac{z}{2}=1\text{.}\) Hint

    Don't let \(z\) be the inside bound.

  4. The region \(D\) that is inside both right circular cylinders \(x^2+z^2=1\) and \(y^2+z^2=1\text{.}\)
