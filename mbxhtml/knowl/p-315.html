Suppose the foci are along the \(y\)-axis (at \((0,\pm c)\)) and the fixed distance \(d\) is now \(d=2b\text{,}\), with vertexes \((0,\pm b)\text{.}\). Let \((a,0)\) be a point on the \(x\) axis that intersect the ellipse. Show that we still have \begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1, \end{equation*} but now we instead have \(a^2+c^2=b^2\text{.}\).
