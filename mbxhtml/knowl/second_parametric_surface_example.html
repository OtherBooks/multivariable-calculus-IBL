Challenge7.3.1More Parametric Surfaces

EXTRA 6 See Sage or Wolfram Alpha. Consider the parametric surface \(\vec r(u,v)=(u\cos v, u\sin v, u^2)\) for \(0\leq u\leq 3\) and \(0\leq v\leq 2 \pi\text{.}\) Construct a graph of this function. To do so, let \(u\) equal a constant (such as 1, 2, 3) and then graph the resulting space curve. Then let \(v\) equal a constant (such as 0, \(\pi/2\text{,}\) etc.) and graph the resulting space curve until you can visualize the surface.
