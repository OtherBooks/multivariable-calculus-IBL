Footnote10.5.9
The quantity \(\int_C x dm\) is sometimes called the first moment of mass about the \(yz\)-plane (so \(x=0\)). Notationally, some people write \(M_{yz} =\int_C x ds\text{.}\). Similarly, we could write \(M_{xz}=\int_C y dm\) and \(M_{xy}=\int_C zdm\text{.}\). With this notation, we could write the center of mass formulas as \begin{equation*} (\bar x,\bar y,\bar z) = \left( \frac{M_{yz}}{m}, \frac{M_{xz}}{m}, \frac{M_{xy}}{m} \right) . \end{equation*} in-context
