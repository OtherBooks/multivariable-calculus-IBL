In the problem above, we showed that \(\vec n d\sigma = \pm(\vec r_x\times\vec r_y)dxdy\) and that \(\vec n d\sigma = \pm(\vec r_r\times\vec r_\theta)drd\theta\text{.}\). We no longer need to find the magnitude of the cross product, but we must determine the correct sign to put on our cross product. This shows us that we can write flux as \begin{equation*} \text{ Flux } =\Phi = \iint_S \vec F\cdot \vec n d\sigma = \iint_{R_{uv}} \vec F\cdot (\pm \vec r_u\times \vec r_v) dudv . \end{equation*}
