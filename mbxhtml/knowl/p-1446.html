In the derivative chapter, we extended differential notation from \(dy=f' dx\) to \(d\vec y = D\vec f d\vec x\text{.}\). The key idea is that a small change in the output variables is approximated by the product of the derivative and a small change in the input variables. As a quick refresher, if we have the function \(z=f(x,y)\text{,}\), then differential notation states that \begin{equation*} dz = \begin{bmatrix}f_x\amp f_y \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}dx\\dy \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
