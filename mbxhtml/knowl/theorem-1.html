Theorem3.2.1The Law of Cosines

Consider a triangle with side lengths \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\text{.}\) Let \(\theta\) be the angle between the sides of length \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) Then the law of cosines states that \begin{equation*} c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos\theta. \end{equation*}

If \(\theta=90^\circ\text{,}\) then \(\cos\theta=0\) and this reduces to the Pythagorean theorem.
