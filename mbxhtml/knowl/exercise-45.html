EXTRA 2 \thomasee{See 12.4: 1-8.} \stewarts{See 12.4:1-7}Let \(\vec u=\langle 1,-2,3\rangle\) and \(\vec v=\langle 2,0,-1\rangle\text{.}\)

  1. Compute \(\vec u\times \vec v\) and \(\vec v\times \vec u\text{.}\) How are they related?

  2. Compute \(\vec u \cdot (\vec u\times \vec v)\) and \(\vec v \cdot (\vec u\times \vec v)\text{.}\) Why did you get the answer you got?

  3. Compute \(\vec u \times (2\vec u)\text{.}\) Why did you get the answer you got?
