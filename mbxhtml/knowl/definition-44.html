Definition12.4.1

Suppose \(\vec T(u,v)=(x(u,v),y(u,v))\) is a differentiable coordinate transformation. The Jacobian of the transformation \(\vec T\) is the absolute value of the determinant of the derivative. EXTRA 1 For a tongue twister, say “the absolute value of the determinant of the derivative” ten times really fast. Notationally we write \begin{equation*} J(u,v) = \frac{\partial (x,y)}{\partial (u,v)} = |\det(D\vec T(u,v))|. \end{equation*}
