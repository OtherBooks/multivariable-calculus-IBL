Let \(Q=(x,y)\) be a point on the hyperbola. By considering the difference of the distances between \(Q\) and the foci, show that an equation of the hyperbola is \(\frac{x^2}{a^2}-\frac{y^2}{c^2-a^2}=1,\) or if we let \(c^2-a^2=b^2\text{,}\), then the equation is \begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{a^2}-\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1. \end{equation*}
