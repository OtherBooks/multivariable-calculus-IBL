Challenge10.3.1Proof of the Fundamental Theorem

EXTRA 7 The proof of the fundamental theorem of line integrals is quite short. All you need is the fundamental theorem of calculus, together with the chain rule (chain_rule_def). Suppose \(f(x,y)\) is continuously differentiable, and suppose that \(\vec r(t)\) for \(t\in[a,b]\) is a parametrization of a smooth curve \(C\text{.}\) Prove that \(f(\vec r(b))-f(\vec r(a)) = \int_a^b Df(\vec r(t))D\vec r(t)\ dt\text{.}\) [If you do this exercise, skip exercise 10.3.6 ]
