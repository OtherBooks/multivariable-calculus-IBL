We want to prove that: \begin{equation*} f(\vec r(b))-f(\vec r(a)) = \int_a^b Df(\vec r(t))D\vec r(t)\ dt \end{equation*}

  1. Let \(g(t) = f(\vec r(t))\text{.}\) Use substitution on the equation. Remember \(t \in [a,b]\text{.}\)

  2. Explain why the equation you get after the substitution is true.

  3. Compute \(g`(t)\) using the matrix form of the chain rule.

  4. Substitute back in to remove \(g(\ )\) from your equation.
