Exercise2.5.2

EXTRA 1 The polynomial you are creating is often called a Taylor polynomial. (I'm giving you the name so that you can search online for more information if you are interested.) Now let \(f(x)=\sin x\text{.}\) Find a 7th degree polynomial so that the function and the polynomial have the same value and same first seven derivatives when evaluated at \(x=0\text{.}\) Evaluate the polynomial at \(x=0.3\text{.}\) How close is this value to your calculator's estimate of \(\sin(0.3)\text{?}\) You may find it valuable to use the notation \begin{equation*} P(x) = a_0+a_1x+a_2x^2+a_3x^3 +\cdots+a_7 x^7. \end{equation*}
