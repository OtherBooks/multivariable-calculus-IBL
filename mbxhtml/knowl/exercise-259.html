Exercise13.1.6

  1. If a surface \(S\) is parametrized by \(\vec r(x,y) = (x,y,f(x,y))\text{,}\) show that \(d\sigma = \sqrt{1+f_x^2+f_y^2}\ dxdy\) (compute a cross product).

  2. If \(\vec r(x,z) = (x,f(x,z),z)\text{,}\) what does \(d\sigma\) equal (compute a cross product - you should see a pattern)?

  3. Use the pattern you've discovered to quickly compute \(d\sigma\) for the surface \(x=4-y^2-z^2\text{.}\)

  4. Set up an iterated double integral that would give the surface area of \(S\) for \(x\geq 0\text{.}\)
