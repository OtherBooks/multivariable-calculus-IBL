Consider the surface \(S\) given by \(z=9-x^2-y^2\text{,}\), for \(z\geq 0\text{.}\). A parametrization of this surface is \begin{equation*} \vec r(x,y) = (x,y,9-x^2-y^2), \text{ where } 9-x^2-y^2\geq 0. \end{equation*}

  1. Give a set of inequalities for \(x\) and \(y\) that describe the region \(R\) over which we need to integrate. The inequalities you give should be in a form that you can use them as the bounds of a double integral.

  2. Find \(d\sigma = \left|\vec r_x\times \vec r_y\right|dxdy\text{.}\)

  3. Set up the surface integral \(\iint_S d\sigma\) as an iterated double integral over \(R\text{.}\)

  4. Convert the integral above to an integral in polar coordinates (don't forget the Jacobian).
