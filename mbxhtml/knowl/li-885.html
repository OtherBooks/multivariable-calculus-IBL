  • We can think of a region \(R\) in the plane as thousands of points \(P(x,y)\text{,}\) each with mass \(dm=\delta dA\text{.}\) As we rotate an entire object about the \(x\)-axis with angular velocity \(\omega\text{,}\) each little piece contributes a small amount of kinetic energy. Explain why the total kinetic energy of the region \(R\text{,}\) when rotated about the \(x\) axis at angular speed \(\omega\text{,}\) is EXTRA 16 The inertia about the \(x\) axis is \(I_x = \ds \iint_R (y^2)dm = \iint_R (y^2)\delta dA\text{.}\) \begin{equation*} KE= \frac{1}{2}\left(\iint_R (y^2)dm\right)\omega^2 \end{equation*} .
