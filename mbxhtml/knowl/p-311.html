Consider the ellipse produced by the fixed distance \(d\) and the foci \(F_1=(c,0)\) and \(F_2=(-c,0)\text{.}\). Let \((a,0)\) and \((-a,0)\) be the vertexes of the ellipse.

  1. Show that \(d=2a\) by considering the distances from \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) to the point \(Q=(a,0)\text{.}\)

  2. Let \(Q=(0,b)\) be a point on the ellipse. Show that \(b^2+c^2=a^2\) by considering the distance between \(Q\) and each focus.

  3. Let \(Q=(x,y)\text{.}\) By considering the distances between \(Q\) and the foci, show that an equation of the ellipse is \begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1. \end{equation*}

  4. Suppose the foci are along the \(y\)-axis (at \((0,\pm c)\)) and the fixed distance \(d\) is now \(d=2b\text{,}\) with vertexes \((0,\pm b)\text{.}\) Let \((a,0)\) be a point on the \(x\) axis that intersect the ellipse. Show that we still have \begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1, \end{equation*} but now we instead have \(a^2+c^2=b^2\text{.}\)
