Exercise3.1.2

EXTRA 1 \thomasee{See 12.1:41-58.} \stewarts{See 12.1:11-18} Recall that a circle (or sphere in 3-D) is just a collection of points which are all equal distance away from a center point.

  1. If the center of a circle is the point \((1,5)\) what is the equation for a circle which passes through the point \((2,3)\text{?}\)

  2. Find the distance between the two points \(P=(2,3,-4)\) and \(Q=(0,-1,1)\text{.}\)

  3. Now find an equation of the sphere passing though point \(Q\) whose center is at \(P\text{.}\) Hint: Each point on the surface of a sphere is \(r\) distance away from the center.
