If you know that a plane passes through the point \((1,2,3)\) and has normal vector \((4,5,6)\text{,}\), then give an equation of the plane. See  8 An equation of the plane is \(4(x-1)+5(y-2)+6(y-3)=0\text{.}\) If \((x,y,z)\) is any point in the plane, then the vector \((x-1,y-2,z-3)\) is a vector in the plane, and hence orthogonal to \((4,5,6)\text{.}\) The dot product of these two vectors should be equal to zero, which is why the plane's equation is \((4,5,6)\cdot (x-1,y-2,z-3)=0\text{.}\) for an answer.
