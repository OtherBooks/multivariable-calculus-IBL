EXTRA 7 \thomasee{See 11.2: 1-14.}Consider the polar curve \(r=1+2\cos \theta\text{.}\) (It wouldn't hurt to provide a quick sketch of the curve.)

  1. Compute both \(dx/d\theta\) and \(dy/d\theta\text{.}\)

  2. Find the slope \(dy/dx\) of the curve at \(\theta=\pi/2\text{.}\)

  3. Give both a vector equation of the tangent line, and a Cartesian equation of the tangent line at \(\theta=\pi/2\text{.}\)
