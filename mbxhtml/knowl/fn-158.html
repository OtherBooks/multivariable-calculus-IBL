We have \(\vec v = \dfrac{d\vec r}{dt} = (-3\sin t, 3\cos t, 4)\text{.}\). The speed is \(\dfrac{ds}{dt}=|\vec v| = \sqrt{9\sin^2t+9\cos^2t+16}=5\text{.}\). We then compute \(\dfrac{d\vec r}{ds}=\dfrac{d\vec r/dt}{ds/dt} = \dfrac{1}{5}(-3\sin t, 3\cos t, 4)\text{,}\), which equals \(\vec T\text{.}\). We finally compute \begin{equation*} \dfrac{d\vec T}{ds}=\dfrac{d\vec T/dt}{ds/dt}=\left(\dfrac{1}{ds/dt}\right)\dfrac{d\vec T}{dt} = \left(\dfrac{1}{5}\right)\dfrac{1}{5}(-3\cos t, -3\sin t, 0) = \dfrac{1}{25}(-3\cos t, -3\sin t, 0). \end{equation*} in-context
