Exercise12.2.7

Consider the region \(R\) that is the circular disc which is inside the circle \((x-2)^2+(y+1)^2=9\text{.}\) The centroid is clearly \((2,-1)\text{,}\) and the area is \(A=\pi(3)^2=9\pi\text{.}\) We can use these fact to simplify many integrals that require integrating over the region \(R\text{.}\)

  1. Compute \(\iint_R 3dA = 3\iint_RdA\text{.}\) [How can area help you?]

  2. Explain why \(\iint_R x dA = \bar x A\) for any region \(R\text{,}\) and then compute \(\iint_R x dA\) for the circular disc. [You don't need to set up any integrals at all.]

  3. Compute the integral \(\iint_R 5x+2y dA\) by using centroid and area facts.
