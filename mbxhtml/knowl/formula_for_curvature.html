Challenge9.5.3

EXTRA 5 See 13.4: 5. The function \(y=f(x)\) can be given the parametrization \(\vec r(x) = (x,f(x))\text{.}\) Use this parametrization (and the previous exercise) to show that the curvature is \begin{equation*} \kappa(x) = \frac{|f''(x)|}{(1+(f')^2)^{3/2}}, \end{equation*} and that the radius of curvature is \begin{equation*} \rho(x) = \frac{(1+(f')^2)^{3/2}}{|f''(x)|}. \end{equation*}
